FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS (FAQ)
TransitFlow Transit Authority

This document answers the most common passenger questions across
all TransitFlow services. Written in plain language for quick reference.

REFUNDS AND CANCELLATIONS
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

Q: Can I get a refund on my National Rail ticket?
A: Yes, as long as you cancel at least 24 hours before your scheduled
departure. A 10% processing fee applies. If you cancel less than
24 hours before departure, the ticket is non-refundable unless there
are exceptional circumstances such as a medical emergency or service
cancellation by TransitFlow.

Q: Can I get a refund on my metro single ticket?
A: No. Metro single tickets are non-refundable once purchased.
Please double-check your journey details before buying.

Q: Can I get a refund on my metro day pass?
A: Yes, but only if you haven't used it yet and you request the refund
within 2 hours of purchase. Once you've tapped in for your first trip,
the day pass cannot be refunded.

Q: My train was cancelled. Can I get my money back?
A: Yes. If a National Rail service is cancelled or delayed by more than
60 minutes due to operational issues, you are entitled to a full refund
with no processing fee. Submit a claim through the app or at a staffed
station within 14 days of the disruption.

Q: How long does a refund take?
A: Refunds are processed within 5 to 7 business days and returned to
your original payment method.

Q: What information do I need to request a refund?
A: You will need your booking ID (format: BK-XXXXXX), your registered
email address, and the reason for the cancellation. For exception-based
refunds, supporting documentation may be required.

TICKETS AND FARES
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

Q: How is my fare calculated?
A: Both metro and National Rail fares use a base fare plus a per-stop
rate. For example, a standard National Rail ticket has a base fare of
$2.50 plus $1.50 for each stop between your origin and destination.
Metro single tickets start at $0.80 with $0.30 per stop.

Q: Is there a cheaper ticket if I travel a lot in one day on the metro?
A: Yes! The metro day pass at $5.00 gives you unlimited trips on all
metro lines (M1, M2, M3, M4) for the whole day. If you're planning
three or more trips, the day pass is usually the better deal.

Q: Do children travel for free?
A: Children under 5 travel free on both metro and National Rail when
accompanied by a fare-paying adult (maximum 2 children per adult).
Children aged 5 to 15 pay half the standard adult fare.

Q: Is there a discount for senior citizens?
A: Yes. Passengers aged 65 and over receive a 30% discount on all
standard class National Rail fares and all metro fares. Please carry
a valid senior citizen card or ID showing your date of birth.

Q: Is there a student discount?
A: Yes. Full-time students get 20% off standard class National Rail
single tickets. You need to select the student fare at booking and
carry a valid student ID. Student discounts do not apply to metro
tickets or first class National Rail tickets.

Q: Can I change my National Rail booking?
A: Booking changes are not supported. You will need to cancel your
existing booking (cancellation fees may apply) and make a new booking
for the service you want.

LUGGAGE AND BELONGINGS
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

Q: How much luggage can I bring?
A: On the metro, you can bring up to 2 bags within standard size limits
(70cm × 50cm × 30cm each). On National Rail, you can bring 2 hand
luggage items plus 1 larger item such as a suitcase.

Q: Can I bring a large suitcase on the metro during rush hour?
A: Oversized items (larger than 70cm × 50cm × 30cm) are not permitted
on metro services during peak hours (07:00–09:00 and 17:00–19:00 on
weekdays). Please plan your travel outside these times if you have
large luggage.

Q: What happens if I leave something on the train?
A: Contact TransitFlow lost property as soon as possible. For the metro,
report to the nearest staffed station. For National Rail, contact
customer support with your booking ID, travel details, and a description
of the lost item. TransitFlow accepts no liability for lost items but
will do its best to reunite passengers with found property.

BICYCLES
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

Q: Can I bring my bike on the metro?
A: Folded bikes are welcome on all metro lines at any time at no charge.
Full-size (unfolded) bikes are allowed on lines M1 and M2 only, outside
peak hours (not between 07:00–09:00 or 17:00–19:00 on weekdays).
Full-size bikes are not allowed on M3 or M4 at any time.

Q: Can I bring my bike on National Rail?
A: Yes, but you need to reserve a bicycle space when booking your ticket.
Spaces are limited (around 4 per service) so book early. Bikes must be
stored in the dedicated bicycle compartment, not in the carriages.

Q: Can I bring an e-bike?
A: E-bikes are treated the same as full-size bicycles. The additional
rule is that the battery must be fully charged and must not be actively
charging during travel.

PETS
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

Q: Can I bring my cat or small dog on the metro?
A: Yes! Small pets in a secure, enclosed carrier are welcome on all
metro lines at any time at no charge. The carrier must fit on your lap
or under your seat.

Q: Can I bring my large dog on the metro?
A: Large dogs (too big for a carrier) are allowed outside peak hours
only. Your dog must be on a lead and muzzled at all times and may not
sit on seats. Only one dog per passenger is permitted.

Q: Can I bring my pet on National Rail?
A: Pets are not permitted on National Rail services, except for
registered assistance dogs, which are welcome at no charge on all
services with no restrictions.

Q: Do I need to pay extra to bring my pet?
A: No. There is no additional charge for bringing a pet (in a carrier)
on the metro. Assistance dogs travel free on all services.

ACCESSIBILITY
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

Q: Are all stations wheelchair accessible?
A: Yes. All 20 metro stations and all National Rail stations on lines
NR1 and NR2 have step-free access via lifts or ramps.

Q: Do I need to book assistance in advance?
A: For National Rail, we recommend booking assistance at least 24 hours
in advance so we can ensure staff and ramps are ready at both ends of
your journey. For the metro, just approach any member of staff on the day.

Q: Is there a discount for carers?
A: Yes. A registered carer travelling with a passenger who requires
full-time support travels free on the same service. You'll need to show
a valid carer's card or equivalent documentation if asked.

Q: Are assistance dogs allowed?
A: Absolutely. Registered assistance dogs are welcome on all metro and
National Rail services at all times, at no charge, with no muzzle
required. They have priority boarding on all services.

STATIONS AND INTERCHANGE
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

Q: How do I travel from the metro to National Rail?
A: The main interchange point is Central Square (MS01) on the metro
and Central Station (NR01) on National Rail — these two stations are
directly connected. Exit the metro fare-paid area and follow the signs
to National Rail. Remember you need a separate ticket for each network.

Q: Which metro station connects to National Rail?
A: Central Square (MS01) is the primary metro station with a direct
interchange to Central Station (NR01) on the National Rail network.

Q: Where can I buy tickets in person?
A: Metro tickets can be purchased at self-service machines at all metro
stations including Central Square (MS01), West Market (MS06), and
Northbridge (MS03). National Rail tickets can only be purchased through
the TransitFlow app or website — there are no walk-up ticket machines
for National Rail at this time.
