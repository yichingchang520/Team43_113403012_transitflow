TICKET TYPES AND FARE INFORMATION
TransitFlow Transit Authority

1. NATIONAL RAIL TICKET TYPES
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

1.1 Standard Class (Single)
Standard class single tickets are available on all National Rail services
including lines NR1 and NR2. The fare is calculated as a base fare plus
a per-stop rate for each stop travelled between the origin and destination.
Standard class seating offers comfortable travel with access to all
shared carriages. Passengers may carry standard luggage within the
permitted limits at no additional charge.

  Base fare: $2.50
  Per-stop rate: $1.50
  Example: Central Station (NR01) to Riverside Park (NR03) = 2 stops
           Total fare = $2.50 + (2 × $1.50) = $5.50

1.2 First Class (Single)
First class single tickets are available on selected National Rail services.
First class coaches (Coach A) offer premium seating with additional
legroom, priority boarding, and a quieter travel environment. First class
tickets must be booked in advance and are subject to seat availability.

  Base fare: $4.00
  Per-stop rate: $2.50
  Example: Central Station (NR01) to Greenfield Junction (NR04) = 3 stops
           Total fare = $4.00 + (3 × $2.50) = $11.50

1.3 Ticket Validity
National Rail single tickets are valid only for the specific service,
date, origin, and destination stated at the time of booking. Tickets
are non-transferable and may not be used by a different passenger.
Tickets are not valid for travel on any other service or date unless
a formal booking change has been completed (note: changes are not
currently supported — see booking rules).

2. METRO TICKET TYPES
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

2.1 Single Ticket
A metro single ticket is valid for one continuous journey between any
two stations on the metro network, including lines M1, M2, M3, and M4.
The fare is calculated as a base fare plus a per-stop rate. Passengers
must complete their journey without exiting and re-entering the network.
Transferring between metro lines (e.g., from M1 to M2 at Central Square
MS01) within a single journey is permitted at no extra charge.

  Base fare: $0.80
  Per-stop rate: $0.30
  Example: Central Square (MS01) to East Gate (MS02) = 1 stop
           Total fare = $0.80 + (1 × $0.30) = $1.10

2.2 Day Pass
A metro day pass provides unlimited travel on all metro lines (M1, M2,
M3, M4) for one full calendar day from the time of first use. The day
pass is ideal for passengers making three or more journeys in a single
day. The pass is valid only for the named passenger and may not be
shared. Day passes are activated automatically upon first use and expire
at midnight on the day of activation.

  Day pass fare: $5.00
  Unlimited trips on all metro lines on the day of activation

2.3 Metro Ticket Validity
Metro single tickets must be used on the day of purchase. They cannot
be deferred to a future date. Day passes are valid for the calendar
day of first activation only and cannot be transferred to another day.

3. SPECIAL PASSENGER FARES
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

3.1 Children (Under 5)
Children under 5 years of age travel free on all metro and National Rail
services when accompanied by a fare-paying adult. No ticket is required.
A maximum of two children under 5 may travel free per accompanying adult.

3.2 Children (Ages 5–15)
Children aged 5 to 15 travel at half the standard adult fare on both
metro and National Rail services. A child fare ticket must be selected
at the time of purchase. Proof of age may be requested by staff or
inspectors at any time during travel.

  Metro single (child): base $0.40 + $0.15 per stop
  National Rail standard class (child): base $1.25 + $0.75 per stop

3.3 Senior Citizens (65 and Over)
Passengers aged 65 or over are entitled to a 30% discount on all
standard class National Rail fares and all metro fares. A valid senior
citizen card or government-issued ID showing date of birth must be
presented upon request. Senior discounts are not available on first
class National Rail tickets.

3.4 Students
Full-time students with a valid student ID from a recognised institution
are entitled to a 20% discount on standard class National Rail fares.
Student discounts apply to single tickets only and must be selected at
the time of booking. Student discounts do not apply to metro tickets
or first class National Rail tickets.

4. PURCHASING TICKETS
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

4.1 How to Buy
Tickets can be purchased through the TransitFlow app or website using a
registered account. Payment is accepted by major credit and debit cards.
Metro tickets and day passes are also available at self-service machines
located at all metro stations including Central Square (MS01), West Market
(MS06), and Northbridge (MS03).

4.2 Ticket Confirmation
Upon successful purchase, passengers receive a booking confirmation by
email containing the booking ID. National Rail passengers must have
their booking ID ready for inspection during travel. Metro passengers
do not need to show a ticket once they have passed through the fare gate.
