ACCESSIBILITY POLICY
TransitFlow Transit Authority

1. STEP-FREE ACCESS
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

1.1 Metro Network
All 20 metro stations are equipped with step-free access via lifts
and/or ramps. Lifts are available at all platforms and connect to
street level and fare-paid areas. Key interchange stations including
Central Square (MS01), West Market (MS06), and Northbridge (MS03)
have multiple lift access points to accommodate high passenger volumes.
In the event of a lift being out of service, signage will direct
passengers to the nearest alternative step-free route. Passengers
requiring assistance should approach any member of station staff.

1.2 National Rail Network
All National Rail stations on lines NR1 and NR2 are equipped with
step-free access. Major stations including Central Station (NR01),
Parklands (NR02), and North Terminal (NR05) have staffed assistance
points on the main concourse. Step-free boarding ramps are available
at all staffed National Rail stations and can be deployed by staff
upon request.

2. WHEELCHAIR AND MOBILITY AID USERS
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

2.1 Wheelchair Spaces on Metro
Each metro carriage is fitted with at least two designated wheelchair
spaces. These spaces are marked clearly and equipped with fold-down
seating that can be raised to accommodate a wheelchair. Passengers
occupying priority spaces are expected to make way for wheelchair
users. Metro services do not require advance notice for wheelchair
users — simply board at any accessible entrance.

2.2 Wheelchair Spaces on National Rail
Each National Rail train includes dedicated wheelchair spaces in both
standard and first class carriages. Passengers using wheelchairs or
large mobility aids are advised to contact TransitFlow at least 24 hours
before their planned journey to ensure a boarding ramp and appropriate
space are reserved. Walk-up wheelchair assistance is available at
staffed stations but is subject to staff availability.

2.3 Mobility Scooters
Mobility scooters that are foldable and within standard luggage size
limits are permitted on all services. Non-foldable mobility scooters
may be permitted on National Rail services subject to space availability
and advance arrangement. Please contact TransitFlow customer support
at least 48 hours before travel to make arrangements.

3. PRIORITY SEATING
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

3.1 Who Priority Seats Are For
Priority seats are reserved for passengers who need them most, including:
  - Passengers with physical disabilities or limited mobility
  - Elderly passengers (65 and over)
  - Pregnant passengers
  - Passengers travelling with young children
  - Passengers recovering from injury or illness

Priority seats are located near the doors on all metro and National
Rail carriages and are clearly marked with the relevant symbols.

3.2 Giving Up Priority Seats
All passengers are expected to offer their priority seat to someone
who needs it. If you are asked to move and are able to do so, please
comply promptly and without hesitation. TransitFlow staff may ask
passengers to vacate priority seats if a passenger with greater need
boards the service.

4. VISUAL AND HEARING IMPAIRMENTS
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

4.1 Visual Impairment
All metro and National Rail stations feature tactile paving to guide
visually impaired passengers from the entrance to the platform. Audio
announcements are made at all stops on metro lines and at all National
Rail stations. The TransitFlow app supports screen readers and
accessibility features on both iOS and Android.

4.2 Hearing Impairment
All metro and National Rail carriages are fitted with visual display
boards showing the next stop and service information. Real-time service
alerts are also available via the TransitFlow app. Staff at staffed
stations are trained to communicate with passengers who are deaf or
hard of hearing. Induction loops are available at all staffed National
Rail ticket offices.

5. REQUESTING ASSISTANCE
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

5.1 Planned Assistance (National Rail)
Passengers requiring assistance boarding or alighting National Rail
services are encouraged to request assistance at least 24 hours before
travel. Requests can be made through the TransitFlow app under
"Accessibility Assistance" or by calling customer support. This ensures
that the appropriate resources and staff are available at both the
departure and arrival station.

5.2 Unplanned Assistance (Metro and National Rail)
If you require unexpected assistance at any station, approach any
member of station staff or use the help point intercom located on
every platform. Staff are available throughout operating hours.
At unstaffed metro stations, the help point connects directly to a
central control room that can coordinate assistance.

5.3 Fare Concessions for Carers
A registered carer travelling with a passenger who requires full-time
support is entitled to travel free of charge on the same service.
Proof of carer status (a valid carer's card or equivalent documentation)
must be presented to staff upon request.
