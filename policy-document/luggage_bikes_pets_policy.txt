LUGGAGE, BICYCLES, AND PETS POLICY
TransitFlow Transit Authority

1. LUGGAGE POLICY
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

1.1 Standard Luggage Allowance (Metro)
Each metro passenger may bring up to 2 items of hand luggage at no
additional charge. Each item must not exceed the following dimensions:
70cm × 50cm × 30cm. Luggage must be stored safely and must not block
aisles, doorways, or priority seating areas. Passengers are responsible
for their own luggage at all times.

Oversized luggage (exceeding the above dimensions) is not permitted
on metro services during peak hours (07:00–09:00 and 17:00–19:00 on
weekdays). Outside peak hours, one oversized item may be carried
provided it does not cause obstruction to other passengers.

1.2 Standard Luggage Allowance (National Rail)
National Rail passengers in standard class may bring up to 2 items of
hand luggage and 1 larger item (e.g. a suitcase) at no additional
charge. Large items must be stored in the designated luggage areas
located at the ends of each carriage. Overhead racks are available
for smaller bags. First class passengers may bring the same allowance
and have access to dedicated overhead storage.

Luggage must not be placed on seats, in aisles, or in emergency exit
areas. TransitFlow staff reserve the right to ask passengers to rearrange
or remove items that pose a safety risk or cause inconvenience to
other passengers.

1.3 Unattended Luggage
Unattended luggage is a security concern. Passengers must not leave
bags or items unattended at any time on either the metro or National
Rail network. Unattended items may be removed by staff and handed to
the relevant authority. TransitFlow accepts no liability for the loss
or damage of unattended luggage.

1.4 Prohibited Items in Luggage
The following items may not be carried on any TransitFlow service:
  - Flammable liquids, gases, or explosives
  - Items with strong odours that may disturb other passengers
  - Items that exceed safe carrying limits in size or weight
  - Any item prohibited under local law

2. BICYCLE POLICY
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

2.1 Folded Bicycles and Scooters (Metro)
Folded bicycles and folded electric scooters are permitted on all metro
lines (M1, M2, M3, M4) at all times at no extra charge. The item must
be fully folded and stored upright beside the passenger. It must not
obstruct aisles or doorways.

2.2 Full-Size Bicycles (Metro)
Full-size (unfolded) bicycles are permitted on metro lines M1 and M2
only, outside of peak hours. Peak hours are defined as 07:00–09:00
and 17:00–19:00 on weekdays (Monday to Friday). Full-size bicycles
are not permitted on metro line M3 or M4 at any time due to carriage
configuration. Passengers with bicycles must use the designated bicycle
spaces available at the end of certain carriages. These spaces are
limited and available on a first-come, first-served basis. No advance
reservation is possible for bicycle spaces on the metro.

2.3 Bicycles on National Rail
Bicycles are permitted on National Rail services on lines NR1 and NR2,
subject to prior reservation. A bicycle reservation must be made at
the time of booking through the TransitFlow app. One bicycle reservation
is permitted per passenger per journey. Bicycle spaces are limited —
typically 4 spaces per service — and are subject to availability.
Bicycles may not be carried in carriages; they must be stored in the
designated bicycle compartments.

If no bicycle space is available for a chosen service, passengers are
advised to consider the next available service with space, or to fold
their bicycle if it is a folding model (see section 2.1).

2.4 E-Bikes and Motorised Cycles
Electric bicycles (e-bikes) are treated as full-size bicycles for the
purposes of this policy, with the additional restriction that e-bike
batteries must be fully charged and not actively charging during travel.
Petrol-powered or motorised cycles of any kind are not permitted on
any TransitFlow service.

3. PET POLICY
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

3.1 Small Pets in Carriers (Metro)
Small pets (cats, small dogs, and similar animals) are permitted on all
metro lines at all times, provided they are in a secure, fully enclosed
carrier. The carrier must be small enough to fit on the passenger's lap
or under the seat and must not block aisles or doorways. There is no
additional charge for carrying a pet in a carrier.

3.2 Dogs on the Metro
Dogs that are too large to fit in a carrier must be kept on a lead and
muzzled at all times during metro travel. Dogs must sit on the floor
and may not occupy seats. Only one dog per passenger is permitted. Dogs
are not allowed to enter the metro during peak hours (07:00–09:00 and
17:00–19:00 on weekdays) unless they are assistance dogs.

3.3 Pets on National Rail
Pets are not permitted on National Rail services, with the exception of
assistance dogs (see section 3.5). Passengers with pets wishing to travel
between National Rail stations are advised to use connecting metro
services where pets are permitted, or to make alternative arrangements.

3.4 Assistance Dogs
Registered assistance dogs — including guide dogs for the visually
impaired, hearing dogs for the deaf, and mobility assistance dogs —
are welcome on all TransitFlow metro and National Rail services at all
times and at no charge. No muzzle is required for assistance dogs.
Assistance dogs must remain under the control of their handler at all
times. Passengers travelling with an assistance dog are entitled to
priority boarding on all services.

3.5 Other Animals
Animals other than domestic pets (e.g. reptiles, birds, farm animals)
are not permitted on any TransitFlow service except in fully enclosed,
secure, and leak-proof containers, subject to the permission of the
station duty manager. TransitFlow reserves the right to refuse carriage
of any animal that may pose a risk to the safety or comfort of other
passengers.
