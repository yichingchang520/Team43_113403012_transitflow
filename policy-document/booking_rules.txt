BOOKING RULES AND RESERVATION POLICY
TransitFlow Transit Authority

1. NATIONAL RAIL BOOKING RULES
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

1.1 Advance Booking Requirement
National Rail seats must be booked at least 30 minutes before the
scheduled departure time. Bookings made less than 30 minutes before
departure will not be accepted by the system. Passengers are strongly
advised to book as early as possible, particularly for peak-hour services
and first class travel, as seats are limited.

1.2 Seat Reservation
Every National Rail booking includes a reserved seat. Passengers are
assigned a specific coach and seat number at the time of booking. The
coach type (A for first class, B for standard) corresponds to the
fare class selected. Passengers must occupy their assigned seat. If a
passenger wishes to sit elsewhere, they must rebook with a different
seat selection, subject to availability.

1.3 Maximum Tickets Per Transaction
A maximum of 9 tickets may be purchased in a single transaction.
For group bookings of 10 or more passengers, please contact TransitFlow
customer support directly. Group bookings are subject to availability
and may require additional lead time.

1.4 Booking Changes
Changes to existing National Rail bookings (including date, time, origin,
destination, or seat) are not currently supported. To travel on a
different service, passengers must cancel the existing booking (subject
to the refund policy) and make a new booking. Cancellation fees may apply.

1.5 Booking Confirmation and ID
Upon completing a National Rail booking, a unique booking ID in the
format BK-XXXXXX is issued. This booking ID must be retained and
presented to staff or inspectors upon request during travel. Passengers
without a valid booking ID may be refused travel or required to purchase
a new ticket on board at the walk-up fare.

1.6 Missed Trains
National Rail tickets are valid only for the specific service booked.
If a passenger misses their train, their ticket is not transferable to
a later service. Passengers must purchase a new ticket for the next
available service. No compensation is provided for missed services
resulting from the passenger's own lateness.

1.7 Operating Days
National Rail services operate seven days a week on lines NR1 and NR2.
Services run between approximately 06:00 and 22:30. Frequency varies
by line; NR1 and NR2 run every 30 minutes during standard hours.
Passengers should check the TransitFlow app for real-time schedule
and any planned engineering works that may affect services.

2. METRO BOOKING RULES
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

2.1 No Advance Booking Required
Metro tickets and day passes do not require advance booking. Passengers
may purchase a ticket or day pass at any time through the TransitFlow
app or at station machines. Seats on the metro are not reserved —
travel is on a first-come, first-served basis.

2.2 Frequency and Operating Hours
Metro lines M1, M2, M3, and M4 operate seven days a week. Services
run every 5 minutes during standard operating hours, from first train
at 05:30 to last train at 23:30. Passengers should check the TransitFlow
app for any planned disruptions or reduced frequency periods.

2.3 Interchange Journeys
Passengers travelling across metro lines may interchange at designated
interchange stations at no additional cost within a single journey.
The key interchange station is Central Square (MS01), which connects
lines M1 and M2. Passengers must not exit the fare-paid area during
an interchange — exiting and re-entering will result in a new fare
being charged.

3. CROSS-NETWORK TRAVEL (METRO + NATIONAL RAIL)
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

3.1 Separate Tickets Required
Metro and National Rail are separate fare systems. Passengers travelling
on a combined journey involving both networks must purchase separate
tickets for each leg. A metro ticket does not permit travel on National
Rail services, and vice versa.

3.2 Interchange Stations
Passengers can transfer between the metro and National Rail networks
at designated interchange stations. The primary interchange point is
Central Square (MS01) and Central Station (NR01), which are directly
connected. Passengers must exit the metro fare-paid area and enter
the National Rail area separately, presenting their National Rail
booking upon entry.

4. ACCOUNT AND REGISTRATION REQUIREMENTS
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

4.1 Account Required for National Rail Bookings
A registered TransitFlow account is required to purchase National Rail
tickets. Registration requires a valid email address, full name, year
of birth, and a secure password. Metro tickets may be purchased as a
guest through station machines, but purchasing through the app requires
a registered account.

4.2 Account Security
Passengers are responsible for keeping their account credentials secure.
TransitFlow will never ask for your password by email or phone. If you
believe your account has been compromised, contact customer support
immediately and change your password via the app.
